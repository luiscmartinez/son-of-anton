### Invariants To Hold

### Attack Surfaces To Probe

1. `FloatingPetInteractionView.hitTest`, `mouseDown`, and subview layout - event-routing attack: can the full-size `SKView` or resize affordance view consume mouse events, making drag/resize inert despite tests passing on pure policy?
2. `FloatingInteractionPolicy.resizeAffordanceRect` and `hitTest` - geometry attack: is the resize target in the expected bottom-right area for AppKit coordinates, and is it small enough not to dominate the pet while still discoverable?
3. `FloatingInteractionPolicy.draggedFrame` and `resizedFrame` - clamp attack: do negative deltas, huge positive deltas, and small visible frames preserve a usable frame without jumping unexpectedly?
4. `FloatingPetController.persistFrameChange` and `saveClampedFrame` - persistence attack: can save failure, hidden state, or repeated `panel.show(frame:)` calls lose the prior state or write an unclamped frame?
5. `FloatingPetController` display-change observer and `reclampForVisibleFrameChange` - lifecycle attack: does an `NSApplication.didChangeScreenParametersNotification` actually re-clamp using the current visible frame and avoid stale off-screen placement?
6. `FloatingPetPanelController.show` and scene presentation - rendering attack: does replacing the content view with `FloatingPetInteractionView` preserve `SKView` transparency, scene presentation, resizing, and current-state updates?
